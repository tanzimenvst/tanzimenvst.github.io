Rooftop Solar Power Potential Assessment

Project Overview

This project demonstrates an end-to-end automated GIS workflow for estimating rooftop solar power generation potential at the neighborhood scale using ArcGIS Pro ModelBuilder.

The tool either processes LiDAR point cloud data to generate high-resolution surface models or use DSM as input and calculates annual solar radiation while accounting for rooftop geometry, shading effects, and sun position throughout the year. Based on defined suitability criteria, it identifies rooftops viable for photovoltaic (PV) installation and estimates building-level electricity production potential.

The workflow aggregates raster-based solar radiation results to individual building footprints, converts solar energy into realistic electrical power output estimates, and applies spatial statistics to identify clusters of high electricity production. These results are further used to support energy infrastructure planning, including the optimal placement of a neighborhood-scale battery storage facility.

All steps are fully automated using ModelBuilder, making the tool reproducible, scalable, and transferable to other cities or study areas with minimal modification.

Project Outcome
  • Building-level estimates of annual solar electricity production
  • Identification of high-potential rooftop clusters
  • Data-driven site selection for battery storage infrastructure
  • A reusable GIS tool suitable for urban renewable energy analysis
Skills Demonstrated

  • Energy potential estimation and unit conversion
  • Spatial decision support for renewable energy planning
